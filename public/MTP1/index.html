Optimizing NOx Conversion Using Machine Learning

Kunal Verma Roll No: 20CH30014 Prof. Suverna Trivedi Department of Chemical Engineering, IIT Kharagpur November 2024

Objective

Conducting laboratory experiments to investigate NOx reactions can be both costly and time-consuming. This is particularly true when determining the optimal concentrations of nitric oxide (NO) and hydrocarbons (HC) to maximize NOx conversion using specific catalysts. To overcome these challenges, this project leverages advanced machine learning techniques to predict the ideal concentrations of NO and HC, along with the optimal reaction temperature. By utilizing predictive modeling, the objective is to achieve higher NOx conversion rates while significantly reducing the reliance on extensive physical testing. This approach aims to save valuable resources, expedite the research process, and facilitate the discovery of more efficient catalytic systems.

Data Collection and Structure

Data points were meticulously gathered from various research publications. Each data point corresponds to a single catalyst, detailing its properties and experimental outcomes, including NOx conversion rates and temperatures at specific NO and HC concentrations.

Dataset Columns

Column Description
Catalyst Composition Main active components and support materials (e.g., Pd, Ce, In).
Support Material Type of support material (e.g., zeolites, metal oxides).
Elemental Composition Key elements in the catalyst, including promoters or additives (e.g., Pd, Ce, Ag, In).
Pore Structure Type of pores (e.g., microporous, mesoporous) and their relevance to catalytic function.
Surface Area (m²/g) Estimated or known surface area; typical range if unavailable.
Pore Volume (cm³/g) Estimated or known pore volume; typical range if unavailable.
Acidity Level of acidity (e.g., high, moderate, low) and its significance for catalytic activity.
Optimal Temperature (K) Temperature range in Kelvin for optimal catalytic performance.
Particle Size (nm) Typical particle size or estimated range.
Key Reactants Main reactants involved in the reaction (e.g., NOx, H₂, CO).
Selectivity (% N₂) Selectivity towards desired product (e.g., percentage selectivity toward N₂ in NOx reduction).
Temperature Stability (K) Maximum temperature at which the catalyst remains stable.
Deactivation Resistance Degree of resistance to deactivation (e.g., high, moderate, low).
Reaction Environment Common reaction conditions.
Support Interaction Type and strength of interaction between active metal and support material.
Electron Transfer Properties Ability of catalyst to facilitate electron transfer (high, moderate, low).
Morphology Surface and bulk structure (e.g., crystalline, amorphous).
Atomic Radius (pm) Size of the primary metal atoms, affecting surface interactions.
Electronegativity Electronegativity values of primary and secondary metals.
Oxidation State Primary oxidation states of the metals in the catalyst.
Support Type Support material type (e.g., zeolites like SSZ-13, ZSM-5; oxides like Al₂O₃, TiO₂).
