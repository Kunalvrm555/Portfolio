Part b: p-Chart for Initial Inspection Phase

Objective: Construct a p-chart for the proportion of nonconforming circuit boards during the initial inspection phase (trial = TRUE), identify samples outside control limits, and discuss potential causes.

Constructing the p-Chart

A p-chart monitors the proportion of nonconforming items in a process. Since each sample consists of 100 circuit boards, the proportion nonconforming (p) is x / 100.

We'll calculate the centerline (average proportion) and control limits (UCL and LCL).

In[42]:
# Calculate proportion nonconforming
initial_phase['p'] = initial_phase['x'] / initial_phase['size']

# Calculate centerline
p_bar = initial_phase['p'].mean()

# Calculate standard deviation for the proportion
sigma_p = (p_bar * (1 - p_bar) / initial_phase['size'].iloc[0]) ** 0.5

# Calculate control limits
UCL = p_bar + 3 * sigma_p
LCL = p_bar - 3 * sigma_p
LCL = max(0, LCL)  # Ensure LCL is not negative

# Plot the p-chart
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.plot(initial_phase['sample'], initial_phase['p'], marker='o', linestyle='-', color='blue', label='Proportion Nonconforming')
plt.axhline(p_bar, color='green', linestyle='--', label='Centerline (p)')
plt.axhline(UCL, color='red', linestyle='--', label='UCL')
plt.axhline(LCL, color='red', linestyle='--', label='LCL')

plt.xlabel('Sample Number')
plt.ylabel('Proportion Nonconforming (p)')
plt.title('p-Chart for Initial Inspection Phase')
plt.legend()
plt.xticks(initial_phase['sample'])
plt.ylim(0, max(initial_phase['p'].max(), UCL + 0.01))
plt.show()

Identifying Out-of-Control Samples

The p-chart displays the proportion of nonconforming circuit boards across the initial inspection phase. Points outside the control limits indicate potential special causes affecting the process.

Out-of-Control Samples:

  • Sample 6: Significant drop in nonconformities due to a new inspector error.
  • Sample 20: Significant increase in nonconformities due to equipment malfunction.

These deviations suggest that the process was unstable during these samples.

Part c: Handling Special Causes and Recalculating Control Limits

Objective: Examine the special causes in Samples 6 and 20, discuss their impact on process stability, and recalculate control limits excluding these points. Then, plot the new control chart.

Impact of Special Causes

  • Sample 6: A new inspector introduced errors, leading to a lower number of nonconformities.
  • Sample 20: Equipment malfunction caused a spike in nonconformities.

These special causes disrupt the stability of the process, making the control limits less representative of the true process performance.

Corrective Actions Taken

  • Sample 6: Additional training was provided to the new inspector.
  • Sample 20: Equipment was repaired and calibrated to prevent future malfunctions.

Recalculating Control Limits Excluding Special Causes

In[43]:
# suppress warnings
import warnings
warnings.filterwarnings('ignore')

# Exclude Samples 6 and 20
clean_initial_phase = initial_phase[~initial_phase['sample'].isin([6, 20])]

# Recalculate centerline and control limits
p_bar_clean = clean_initial_phase['p'].mean()
sigma_p_clean = (p_bar_clean * (1 - p_bar_clean) / clean_initial_phase['size'].iloc[0]) ** 0.5
UCL_clean = p_bar_clean + 3 * sigma_p_clean
LCL_clean = p_bar_clean - 3 * sigma_p_clean
LCL_clean = max(LCL_clean, 0)

# Add new control lines
clean_initial_phase['Centerline'] = p_bar_clean
clean_initial_phase['UCL'] = UCL_clean
clean_initial_phase['LCL'] = LCL_clean

# Plot the new p-chart
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.plot(clean_initial_phase['sample'], clean_initial_phase['p'], marker='o', linestyle='-', color='blue', label='Proportion Nonconforming')
plt.axhline(p_bar_clean, color='green', linestyle='--', label='Centerline (p)')
plt.axhline(UCL_clean, color='red', linestyle='--', label='UCL')
plt.axhline(LCL_clean, color='red', linestyle='--', label='LCL')

plt.xlabel('Sample Number')
plt.ylabel('Proportion Nonconforming (p)')
plt.title('Recalculated p-Chart Excluding Samples 6 and 20')
plt.legend()
plt.xticks(clean_initial_phase['sample'])
plt.ylim(0, max(clean_initial_phase['p'].max(), UCL_clean + 0.01))
plt.show()

New Control Chart

By excluding Samples 6 and 20, the recalculated control limits better represent the stable process. The new p-chart shows that all remaining samples are within control limits, indicating a stable process after corrective actions.

Part d: c-Chart for Follow-Up Inspection Phase

Objective: Analyze the follow-up inspection phase (trial = FALSE) using a c-chart for nonconformities per unit (100 boards), compare the control limits to those in the initial phase (excluding Samples 6 and 20), and discuss process improvement.

In[44]:
# Prepare follow-up phase data
followup_phase = followup_phase.copy()
followup_phase['c'] = followup_phase['x']

# Calculate centerline
c_bar_followup = followup_phase['c'].mean()

# Calculate control limits
UCL_c = c_bar_followup + 3 * (c_bar_followup ** 0.5)
LCL_c = c_bar_followup - 3 * (c_bar_followup ** 0.5)
LCL_c = max(LCL_c, 0)  # LCL cannot be negative

# Add control lines to the DataFrame
followup_phase['Centerline'] = c_bar_followup
followup_phase['UCL'] = UCL_c
followup_phase['LCL'] = LCL_c

# Plot the c-chart
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.plot(followup_phase['sample'], followup_phase['c'], marker='o', linestyle='-', color='purple', label='Nonconformities (c)')
plt.axhline(c_bar_followup, color='green', linestyle='--', label='Centerline (c)')
plt.axhline(UCL_c, color='red', linestyle='--', label='UCL')
plt.axhline(LCL_c, color='red', linestyle='--', label='LCL')

# Highlight points outside control limits
out_of_control_followup = followup_phase[(followup_phase['c'] > UCL_c) | (followup_phase['c'] < LCL_c)]
plt.scatter(out_of_control_followup['sample'], out_of_control_followup['c'], color='orange', s=100, label='Out of Control')

plt.xlabel('Sample Number')
plt.ylabel('Number of Nonconformities (c)')
plt.title('c-Chart for Follow-Up Inspection Phase')
plt.legend()
plt.xticks(followup_phase['sample'])
plt.ylim(0, max(followup_phase['c'].max(), UCL_c + 1))
plt.show()
