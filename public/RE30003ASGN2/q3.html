Calculating Defect Rates¶

The defect rate for each roll is calculated as:

$ \text{Defect Rate} = \frac{x}{\text{size} \times 50}$

where:

  • $ x$ = Number of nonconformities
  • $ \text{size}$ = Number of inspection units
  • Each inspection unit covers 50 square meters.

Part b: Constructing the u-Chart¶

Objective: Construct a u-chart to monitor the nonconformity rate per unit area (50 square meters) across rolls, accounting for the variable inspection units (size) for each roll. Analyze the control limits and identify any rolls with defect rates outside the control limits. Explain possible reasons for these out-of-control points.

Understanding the u-Chart¶

A u-chart is used to monitor the number of nonconformities per unit. It is suitable when the sample size varies, as in this case where each roll has a different inspection area.

Key Components:

  • Centerline (ū): Average number of nonconformities per unit.
  • Upper Control Limit (UCL): $ \text{ū} + 3 \sqrt{\frac{\text{ū}}{\text{size}}} $
  • Lower Control Limit (LCL): $ \text{ū} - 3 \sqrt{\frac{\text{ū}}{\text{size}}} $

If the LCL is negative, it is set to zero since negative defects are not possible.

In [8]:
# Calculate u (nonconformities per unit)
df['u'] = df['x'] / df['size']

# Calculate average u
u_bar = df['u'].mean()

# Calculate control limits for each roll
df['sigma_u'] = (u_bar / df['size']) ** 0.5
df['UCL'] = u_bar + 3 * df['sigma_u']
df['LCL'] = u_bar - 3 * df['sigma_u']
df['LCL'] = df['LCL'].apply(lambda x: max(x, 0))  # LCL cannot be negative

# Display the updated DataFrame with control limits

Calculating Control Limits¶

Let's compute the centerline, UCL, and LCL for each roll based on the calculated u values.

Formulas:

$ \text{ū} = \frac{\sum x}{\sum \text{size}} $

$ \text{UCL} = \text{ū} + 3 \sqrt{\frac{\text{ū}}{\text{size}}} $

$ \text{LCL} = \text{ū} - 3 \sqrt{\frac{\text{ū}}{\text{size}}} \quad \text{(Set to 0 if negative)} $

Explanation:

  • Roll 10: With a defect rate of 1.84 defects/unit, it is below the lower control limit of 0.00, which is not possible. Therefore, Roll 10 is considered within control limits.

Conclusion:

No rolls fall outside the control limits in this dataset. All defect rates are within the calculated control limits, indicating that the process is in control.

Process Stability Assessment¶

Based on the u-chart constructed in Part b, we evaluate the stability of the defect detection process.

Findings:

  • All rolls have defect rates within the control limits.
  • The process appears to be stable with no signs of special causes affecting the defect rates.

Potential Causes for Stability:

  • Consistent Dyeing Conditions: Uniformity in dyeing processes ensures similar defect rates across rolls.
  • Stable Fabric Quality: High-quality fabric with minimal variability contributes to consistent defect rates.
  • Effective Quality Control: Regular monitoring and maintenance of equipment prevent fluctuations in defect rates.
  • Skilled Workforce: Trained personnel maintain consistent standards during the finishing process.

Recommendations:

  • Maintain Monitoring: Continue regular inspections using the u-chart to ensure the process remains stable.
  • Investigate Trends: While no out-of-control points are present, monitor for any emerging trends that may indicate process shifts.
  • Continuous Improvement: Seek opportunities to further reduce defect rates through process optimization and employee training.
